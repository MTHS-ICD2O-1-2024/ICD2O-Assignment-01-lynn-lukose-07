Margaret Hamilton

1936-Present Day

There was no choice but to be pioneers.

Margaret Hamilton is an American computer scientist, famous for making contributions to the code that helped land humans on the moon.

1936

Margaret Hamilton was born on August 17, 1936 in Paoli, Indiana.

1958

Margaret recieved her Bachelors degree in Mathematics and Philosophy from Earlham College in 1958.

1959

Margaret took up a research position in Massachussets, at MIT. She began programming software to predict weather, and did postgraduate work on meteorology.

1960's

Margaret joined MIT's Lincloln Laboratory, which was involved in the first U.S. air defense system. She wrote some of the code to detect enemy aircraft.

1960's

Margaret joined MIT's instrumentation Laboratory. She was the leader of a team tasked with developing the software for the guidance and control systems of the modules for the Apollo missions. She focused on software used to detect system errors and to find information in case of a computer crash which proved helpful in the Apollo 11 mission.

1976

She cofounded a company called Higher Order Software. There, she created a program called USE.IT, which was successfully implemented into many government programs. She was CEO of the company through 1984, and left in 1985.

1986

In March 1986, she founded Hamilton Technologies, Inc. at Cambridge Massachussets.

2016

She received the Presidential Medal of Freedom, one of the highest civillian honours in the United States.

Fun Facts

  • She was inducted into the National Aviation Hall of Fame in Dayton, Ohio.
  • There were zero bugs in her software code for the Apollo missions.
  • She was the first person to use the term 'software engineer'.
